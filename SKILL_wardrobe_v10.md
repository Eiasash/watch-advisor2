## PAIRING PRINCIPLES

### Pattern grammar (not color matching)
- **Clous de Paris / hobnail dials** (GP Laureato, VC Overseas rep, Breguet Tradition guilloché) → pair with **small-scale gridded fabrics**: Prince of Wales check, glen plaid, nailhead, bird's-eye. Grid-on-grid is the structural logic — works regardless of dial color.
- **Smooth-dial dress watches** (Reverso, Pasha, Santos) → pair with **smooth worsted or solid-textured suits** where no competing pattern exists.
- **Sector / striped dials** (Hanhart Pioneer) → pair with **subtle tonal textures** (herringbone, flannel), not loud patterns.

### Formal context rules
- **Double-breasted suits REQUIRE a tie.** Open collar under DB at a wedding reads as undercutting the formality the DB was chosen to deliver. Single-breasted is more forgiving.
- **Black oxfords > black derbies** with any suit formality 9+ (Kiral DB). Derbies are fine with f8 and below (Pavarotti).
